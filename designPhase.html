  • First Prototype

    CMD Method: Prototyping



    After reviewing the designs that each of us created, we decided to combine the best elements from them into a single concept. Using the selected components, we created a basic prototype that can be used in AB testing. We focused on key functionalities and user interactions while designing the first prototype.

  • Second Prototype

    CMD Method: Prototyping



    By analysing the collected data and user responses from the A/B testing, we gained insights into what worked well and what needed improvement. Using this feedback, we developed a second prototype that addressed the identified issues and incorporated the suggested improvements.

    Features and pages

    In the end our last prototype consists of 7 pages. When the users open the app they will be shown real time data of Tension level, HRV and Sleep analysis in the Overview page. When users want to get more insights into their tension level they can press on it and go to the Tension Page where they can see the lowest and highest tension level of the day and which activity they did during those peaks of stress. Swiping up will bring them to the Calendar Page where where they can see their tension level along the day with time stamps and events added by the users themselves. This is one of the app’s way of showing potential stressors to the users.

    When clicking on the Calendar Icon in the navigation menu, the monthly view page will appear. Here users will get a heat map in the form of a calendar. Each day of the month will show their average tension level. Another feature of the app is the Activities Insights which show the activities that created the most tension for the user.

    If users want to see more information regarding a very high tension day they can press it and will be given more in depth look at the tension levels of the day and which activities and events they went through. Users can also pin events on the heatmap and leave notes if they want to write the reason they had low or high tension.

  • Information Arhitecture

    During the design phase we created an Information Architecture to showcase what pages our app has and to show how they relate to each other within the system.

  • C4 Model

    The C4 Model is used to visualize the architecture of a software system. The diagram has 4 steps: Context, Container, Component and Code.

    The C4 Model is used to communicate to certain people (stakeholders, programmers) based on each step what the app is and what it contains and it is used to document the different components and their relationship within the system.

    As a team we created the C4 model to communicate our app’s layout to the stakeholder and to show to future students working on this project how the app actually would work.

    Since we did not program the application the last level of the C4 Model was not added.

    I worked on the C1 and C3 because I do not have knowledge of how API and Database work. However after seeing how my teammates worked on C2 model I learned more about it.
