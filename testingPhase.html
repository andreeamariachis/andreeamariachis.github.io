Testing Phase

  • Introduction

    To validate our designs, we conducted an AB testing with the first prototype we created. AB testing is a method used to compare two versions of a webpage or app to determine which one performs better in terms of user engagement or based on user requirements.

    CMD Method: AB Testing

  • Process

    Each one of us conducted an AB testing with different users via a survey or online and in person.

    My testing was conducted online and I showed to the participant all the designs one by one while encouraging the user talk out loud and see if he understands or not the graphs we created. In the end the participant had to choose the designs that made the most sense to him.

    We gathered very good feedback from our participants that can be used in the iteration of our next prototype.

  • Results from my AB testing

    This is some of the feedback on the first Overview Page of our prototype given by participant 3:

    The participant understood that the first graph shows real time data of tension level. But was confused by the “mm” term. He likes the “Incoming” feature that shows him his next event taken from his calendar. The participant was not sure what 60% efficiency means. He expects he can get more information if he presses on the card to go to the Sleep Page. The participant expressed confusion when looking at the clock graph since it did not have accurate data and no explanation for what each color means.

  • Conclusion

    In conclusion, the feedback from participants highlights the importance of clarity, meaningful explanations, intuitive design, and the provision of additional information for a better understanding of the data visualizations. Incorporating these suggestions can enhance the user experience and make the screens more informative and user-friendly.
